# Part 2 · The Case

> Why the idea is sound and why the window is now: eighty years of lineage, four preconditions that finally hold, the empirical base with grades attached, and the naming vacancy stated precisely.
> Evidence grades: ✅ verified (three-vote adversarial or primary-source word-for-word) · ◈ cross-confirmed by multiple batches · ◎ corrected wording · ◐ single source · ⚠️ unverified. Facts as of 2026-07-09.

## 1. Eighty years in one page

The idea is not new; the *window* is. The lineage compresses into a pendulum: **the dream of the user's delegate (1945–1998) → capture by platform algorithms (2000–2022) → the delegate becomes possible again (2023–)**.

**The dreamers.** Bush's Memex (1945 ✅) imagined a machine for consulting existing knowledge along personal associative trails — the entire essay is about aiding consumption, not production. Simon (1971 ◈) supplied the field's first principle of economics: *a wealth of information creates a poverty of attention* — twenty years before the web. Apple's Knowledge Navigator concept film (1987 ◈): most of what the bow-tied butler Phil does is consume information *for* the professor.

**The builders.** MIT's Fishwrap (1993 ✅) ran the first working personalized newspaper — and, alert to the failure mode in year one, deliberately kept a shared **"Daily We"** front page; thirty years later, every discussion of common-ground modes is relearning that lesson. Maes's interface agents (CACM 1994 ✅) laid the academic foundation — *indirect management*, agents that watch, imitate, take feedback; all four prototypes were consumption scenarios (email, news filtering, music recommendation, entertainment picks), and the motive is in the title: information overload. Her collaborative filtering commercialized as Firefly (acquired by Microsoft, 1998). Lieberman's Letizia (1995 ◐) watched you browse and explored links ahead of you — the earliest running prototype of "the agent consumes part of it first." Negroponte's Daily Me (1995 ✅) named the vision in words that nearly coincide with this framework — *"your interface agent can read every newswire and newspaper… then construct a personalized summary… printed in an edition of one"* — and even imagined a **personalization knob**, the 1995 ancestor of the autonomy slider. *The Diamond Age*'s Young Lady's Illustrated Primer (1995 ◈) became education's north star — and **the loyalty problem is already written into the novel: the Primer's educational goals are set by whoever commissioned it.** The generation failed for three reasons: no AI that understood meaning, collaborative filtering that only knew "people like you also liked," no business model. **But the shape of the dream was consumption-side from the very beginning.**

**The academic detour.** Brusilovsky's adaptive hypermedia (1996–2007 ◐) made "adjust presentation to a user model" a formal field, with clear positive findings (adaptive presentation improves comprehension) — and a fatal bottleneck: **every adaptation required manually pre-authored variants**. The field was absorbed by recommender systems, because selection scales and re-presentation did not. **That is exactly the variable LLMs deleted: generation cost collapsed, and adaptive presentation is alive again.** The vacuum was filled by platforms — News Feed (2006), Pariser names the filter bubble (2011), TikTok perfects the form (2016): platform consumption agents proved the demand exists and demonstrated the complete degradation path of misaligned loyalty. The resistance in between: Searls's intention economy (2012 ◈ — "customers arrive carrying their own data, terms, and intent"; for over a decade, protocols without an execution body); BBC Object-Based Media (2013– ◐ — production-grade separation of semantic and experience layers, which also exposed the combinatorial-explosion ceiling of pre-authored variants); Fukuyama's middleware proposal (2020 ◐) and Bluesky's custom feeds (2023–).

**The LLM era: five columns converging on the same summit.**

- **Web-standards circle (loyalty ethics):** Doctorow's *loyal user agent* (2024 ◎) — platform-era apps are "treacherous user agents," and the antidote is fiduciary duty; Unternet ("we need a new, modern user agent"); Consumer Reports' *Loyalty by Design*.
- **HCI circle (rendering methodology):** Wattenberger — "chat is a shapeless tool" (2023); Linus Lee's pinch-to-summarize prototypes (2022 ✅); Appleton's prediction of personal filter agents — *browsing the raw web without your model filtering it will come to seem absurd* (✅); **Nielsen's two generations of generative UI (2024-02 ◐) — design-time vs. consumption-time, structurally the closest published precedent to this framework**; Ink & Switch's *Malleable Software* (2025 ✅ — the other wing of the same user-sovereignty movement: users reshaping tools, agents reshaping content).
- **Platform & infrastructure circle (the supply-side mirror):** Karpathy — *"99.9% of attention is about to be LLM attention"* (2025-03 ✅). He says content should be written for agents to consume; this framework says agents should render for humans to consume. Put together, they form the full pipeline: semantic layer → compiler → experience layer. Microsoft's NLWeb ("NLWeb is to the agentic web what HTML was to the web" — authored by Guha, father of RSS and Schema.org ✅).
- **Economics circle (the pricing logic):** NBER's *The Coasean Singularity?* (2025-11 ◎) — the first systematic consumer-side economics of agents; Microsoft Research's *The Agentic Economy* (2025-05 ◎) proposes assistant vs. service agents — **the closest partial precedent to date, but split by whom the agent represents, not by the production/consumption axis**; Gartner's machine-customer forecast ($30T ⚠️ second-hand).
- **Chinese-language context:** "from 'pushed to me' to 'AI finds it for me'" (2025-05 ◐); but the local term has been captured by companionship apps and agentic commerce — **in the Chinese-speaking world the concept is unclaimed and the product slot unoccupied.**

**Three lessons from the lineage.** Ideas were never scarce — bottlenecks fell one per generation, and only context sensing and settlement remain. Every vacuum in user delegation got filled by platforms (recommender systems *are* platform-owned consumption agents); if this generation stalls too, the fillers will be Atlas, Gemini, and their peers — platform delegates a hundred times stronger than News Feed. **The most dangerous rival is not "nobody thought of it" but "the platforms thought of it too."** And side effects get predicted in advance, then happen on schedule — the risk list is a statistical regularity, to be treated as design input.

## 2. Why now: four preconditions

1. **Technology.** Multimodal generation crossed the quality bar (human-parity TTS, real-time UI code generation); long context; inference cheap enough that *per-person rendering is economically sane*; on-device models (the privacy precondition).
2. **Demand.** Production agents drove the marginal cost of content to zero → the AI-slop flood (non-human traffic past half on Cloudflare's measure ◎) → the scarce resource has fully shifted to the attention side. **The first half automated speaking; the second half must automate listening.**
3. **Infrastructure.** MCP/A2A converged (how agents acquire material and cooperate); three presentation-layer protocols within a year (see [Part 3](03-the-landscape.md)); settlement rails being laid (HTTP 402, x402, RSL).
4. **Evidence.** For the first time, both load-bearing hypotheses — *re-rendering works* and *sycophancy harms* — have data. See below.

## 3. The empirical base (all figures post-verification)

| Claim | Data | Source & grade |
|---|---|---|
| People prefer generated interfaces over text | win-rate **82.8%** vs. Markdown, **97%** vs. plain text, 90% vs. the query's top search result; "at least comparable to human experts" 50% of the time (35.3% win + 14.7% tie); judged an **emergent capability** | Google GenUI paper, Table 2; LMArena set; generation latency excluded ◎ |
| Generated UI vs. chat UI | human preference up to **+72%** (largest gains on information-dense tasks; casual chat remains competitive — drawing the boundary of *when rendering is worth it*) | Stanford SALT, ACL 2026 ✅ |
| Re-rendering lifts learning retention | retention test at 3–5 days: **78% vs. 67% (+11 pp)**; immediate quiz +9% (RCT, 60 students aged 15–18, control = digital PDF reader) | Google Learn Your Way ◎ |
| Presentation preferences differ by person | 20 designers × 600 pairwise UI judgments: agreement only **κ = 0.25** | arXiv:2604.09876 ◐ |
| Good summaries differ by person | PersonalSum dataset: personalized summarization remains unsolved | arXiv:2410.03905 ◐ |
| Sycophancy harms, challenge helps | conversational search (even neutral) increases confirmatory querying and polarization; sycophantic style worsens it, challenging style mitigates | CHI 2024, *Generative Echo Chamber?* ◐ |
| Cognitive load is a real driver | 78.5% of users who mention cognitive load prefer generative UI; load-aware streaming saves 16.8% compute | ACL 2026 / UIST 2025 ◐ |
| Accessibility gains are largest | reading difficulties (~12% of population): the more severe, the more adaptation helps | CHI 2025 LARF ◐ |
| Presentation changes behavior | Spotify AI DJ: with voice narration, users accept unfamiliar tracks they would otherwise skip | ◐ |
| The cognitive cost of mediated consumption | weakest EEG connectivity in the LLM group; 83% could not quote what they had just written; "cognitive debt" persists (small sample; the task was *writing* — analogize with care; **Pattie Maes is a co-author: the lineage critiques itself**) | MIT, arXiv:2506.08872 ◐ |
| Mediated consumption drains upstream traffic | with AI summaries present, link clicks fall 15%→8%, citation clicks 1%, 66% of sessions end with zero visits (Pew, 68,879 real searches); publisher Google traffic median −10%; **counter-evidence duly kept**: zero-click was already the norm (~2/3), and users are adapting (45%→38%) | ◐ — cite both directions |

**An honesty note (added after external review).** Every hard number above supports **L2 (transformation) and the presentation layer** — precisely the layer most absorbable by platforms. The differentiation this framework bets on — **L3: timing, budgets, triage — has, as of this writing, zero positive empirical results** (Pulse is a negative sample; ProactiveBench is an evaluation framework, not an effect size; "no benchmark for consumption quality" is research gap #1 in [Part 5](05-open-territory.md)). *The evidence is thickest where the moat is thinnest, and the moat is deepest where the evidence is empty.* Whoever produces the first positive L3 result — probably via a self-run attention-budget experiment — is supplying the missing keystone.

## 4. The naming vacancy, stated precisely

Four independent searches (≈20 bilingual query sets, plus three independent external confirmations, as of 2026-07-09): **no source names an agent typology with production-vs-consumption as its first-class axis** ✅. The nearest misses triangulate from three *different* axes, none of them value-flow: Microsoft's assistant/service agents (by **whom the agent represents** ◎), Nielsen's two GenUI generations (by **binding time** ◐), NBER's demand-side/supply-side agents (by **market role** ◎). False friends to discard: "production/consumption" in agent-economy supply-chain writing (industry-chain axis), the Chinese "consumer-grade agent" (market-tier axis), BI's "consumption agent" (semantic-model querying), energy's "peak consumption agent."

**The judgment.** Mature components mean feasibility has been verified piecemeal; a naming vacancy means the definition rights are still unassigned; term erosion means the window is closing. Historically, the person who sees the gestalt and names it tends to capture more than the person who builds the first fragment.
