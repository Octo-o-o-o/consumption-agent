# The Twelve Takeaways

> Part 0 of the **Consumption Agents** handbook — the five-minute layer. Each claim is expanded in the chapters that follow.
> Evidence grades used throughout: ✅ adversarially verified or checked against primary sources · ◎ corrected wording after dedicated verification (do not quote older figures) · ◐ single source with a primary link · ○ reasoning/judgment · ⚠️ could not be verified. All facts as of **2026-07-09**.

1. **The dichotomy is an unclaimed naming vacancy — and the window is narrowing** ✅. Four independent literature searches found no one who names an agent typology along the production/consumption axis. The nearest partial precedents are Microsoft's *assistant vs. service agents* (split by **who is represented** ◎) and Nielsen's two generations of generative UI (split by **binding time**: design-time vs. consumption-time ◐). Meanwhile the phrase "consumption agent" is being nibbled away by vertical niches (BI semantic-model querying, energy analytics). The definition rights are still available; they will not stay available.

2. **The lineage is eighty years old; five generations hit five different walls** ✅. Memex (1945) → Maes's interface agents (1994) → Negroponte's Daily Me (1995 — the original text literally reads *"your interface agent can read every newswire… construct a personalized summary"*) → adaptive hypermedia (1996) → the intention economy (2012) → five converging columns in the LLM era. Each generation died of a different bottleneck (no technology / no language understanding / no way to generate variants / no execution body). **LLMs dissolve "understanding + generation" in one stroke; what remains is context sensing and settlement.**

3. **The critique is as old as the dream.** Sunstein predicted the echo chamber (2001) before the thing was even built; it then happened on schedule (filter bubbles, 2011). The risk list is a statistical regularity of history — treat it as design input, not as a PR problem.

4. **2025-11-18 was the industry's inflection point** ✅. Google shipped generative interfaces with Gemini 3 (custom UI generated per prompt); the same day, Google Research published the institutional definition of *interface-as-output* — and Google split task agents and presentation interfaces into two product lines. Big-tech product structure already mirrors the dichotomy; only the name is missing.

5. **The empirical foundation is already thick enough** ◎✅. Generative interfaces win 82.8% against Markdown and 97% against plain text, judged "at least comparable to human experts" 50% of the time, and are classified as an emergent capability; re-rendering form lifts learning retention by 11 percentage points (78% vs. 67% at 3–5 days); designers agree on UI preferences at only κ = 0.25 (one-size-fits-all is false); sycophantic conversational search measurably *worsens* confirmation bias while challenging search mitigates it (the "coach" stance has data).

6. **Every layer has players; nobody runs the whole stack** ◈. L2 transformation has been platformized (NotebookLM's multi-format hub), L1 curation has boutique players (Particle's liquid content), L3 orchestration exists only in fragments. In the capability matrix (context sensing × preference modeling × modality transformation) **no product fills a single row** — what is missing is the scheduling intelligence that knows *what to compile this content into, right now*. That gap is the opportunity.

7. **Three presentation-layer protocols appeared within one year** ✅ — AG-UI (event delivery), A2UI (declarative rendering), MCP Apps (sandboxed iframes, co-drafted by Anthropic **and** OpenAI) — plus WebMCP going the reverse direction (agents entering the page a human is on). "How an agent presents to a human" has been institutionalized as a standalone protocol problem: industry-level acknowledgment that the consumption side is its own domain. Independent products should reuse this layer and differentiate on the taste passport and loyalty architecture.

8. **The content economy is being rebuilt for agent-mediated consumption** ✅◎. Cloudflare reports non-human traffic past half (57.5% of HTML requests, its own network's measure); Pay Per Crawl is becoming Pay Per Use; RSL 1.0 launched with 1,500+ endorsing institutions; Comet Plus is the first revenue formula with "agent actions" as a line item; Reuters runs a paid MCP server. Yet llms.txt shows "97% of files receive zero AI requests" ◐ — **the supply side is prepping meals far faster than the demand side is ordering. Consumption agents at scale are the demand engine the whole pipeline is waiting for.** That is the leverage position.

9. **Loyalty is the first-order question, and the business model decides it in advance** ◈ (all five research batches converged independently). Dealer / butler / coach — who pays determines which one you get; B2B adds a fourth failure mode, the **overseer** (organization pays, throughput gets optimized instead of cognitive well-being). And money is only one channel: **the training signal is the second** — behavioral signals (taps, skips, completions) run the same gradient as engagement optimization, so dealer dynamics can grow out of the data loop without any advertising. Ads are a death sentence; user-funded + on-device + auditable is the only soil for a loyal agent — which is precisely what attention-monetizing incumbents structurally cannot build, and the only door left open for new entrants. Butler value carries the commercial floor; coach is the upside differentiation.

10. **The casualty list gives three causes of death; every survivor answers one** ◎. Proactivity by silent inference (ChatGPT Pulse, sunset 2026-06 — users don't reject proactivity, they reject proactivity *they don't control*); no commercial loop (Instant Checkout, terminated 2026-03 — trust is granted per-step); indefensible independence (Limitless and Bee acquired; Dia sold for $610M). Survivors: Snipd respects the original form, Particle builds in creator revenue-share, Kagi sells restraint. **The legal boundary is live**: the Amazon v. Perplexity preliminary injunction is stayed on appeal; no final ruling.

11. **Seven vacancies** (post-correction): the taste passport (◎ Stanford's Loyal Agents project already has the HCP proposal but no product adoption — the competitive signal is lit; what remains unclaimed is the reference implementation and a consumption-specific ontology), the **intent compiler** (state → rendering parameters; completely empty — *upgraded to first priority: it is the interpreter, and the passport is merely its memory; what can be carried away is data, what cannot is the interpreter*), timing & cadence, the agent firewall, the slow-consumption companion, group consumption / common ground, and the non-English (Chinese-ecosystem) niche. Plus two cross-cutting layers: **metrics** (an attention-ROI indicator family) and **interface** (the artifact contract — production-agent output should carry consumption metadata).

12. **Three non-negotiable design axioms**, derived by inverting the full tension list: **auditable loyalty** (prove it with architecture, not promises — and audit *both channels*: where the money flows and what signals the model learns from), **anchor-back** (every rendering links back to its canonical source in one step), and **knowing when not to transform** (form-preserving judgment plus an anti-doomscroll discipline). They cash out as eight red lines, including the B2B loyalty firewall.
