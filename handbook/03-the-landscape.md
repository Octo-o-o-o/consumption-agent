# Part 3 · The Landscape

> The state of the world: who builds what, who died of what, which bricks exist in open source, what academia knows, and how the protocol and settlement layers are forming.
> Evidence grades: ✅ verified · ◎ corrected wording (do not quote older figures) · ◐ single source · ⚠️ unverified. Facts as of 2026-07-09; the product layer decays fastest — revisit before relying on it.

## 1. The product map

**The inflection point: 2025-11-18.** Google ships generative interfaces with Gemini 3 (magazine-style *visual layout* + *dynamic view* that writes custom UI per prompt ✅ — Labs-flagged, adoption unproven); Google Research publishes *interface-as-output* the same day; and Google splits task agents and presentation interfaces into two product lines. The industry's product structure now mirrors the production/consumption dichotomy — unnamed.

**Layer by layer:**

- **Entry layer (browsers & OS)** — the fight for the *consumption delegate* position itself. OpenAI's Atlas (browser-as-agent, browser memories, logged-out mode as injection defense); Perplexity's Comet (most aggressive autonomy — and hence first to be sued, see §3); The Browser Company's Dia (the restrained *reading-side* route — **sold to Atlassian for $610M in cash, 2025-09**: the route closest to this framework, and its sale says independent browsers can't sustain; entry scarcity has been priced by capital); Microsoft Edge Copilot Mode; Chrome auto-browse (the largest-scale agentification, sinking into the Android system layer through 2026); Apple Intelligence notification summaries (the OS-level cautionary tale — see R1 in [Part 4](04-risks-and-red-lines.md)). Four bets in one sentence: Atlas bets *entry = assistant*, Comet bets *entry = executor*, Dia bet *entry = reader*, Apple bets *OS = filter* — all four take rendering ownership back from the website.
- **Proactive-feed layer (the first round of L3 trial and error)** — ChatGPT Pulse ◎: launched 2025-09-25 (overnight research → morning cards), **sunset announced 2026-06-17, removed early 2026-07, folded into user-managed Scheduled Tasks**. Its design language deserves the record: one batch per day, 24-hour expiry, an explicit "deliberately unlike engagement feeds" stance — the first big-lab product to talk like an attention fiduciary. The official epitaph: proactive experiences work when "personalized, action-oriented, and **steerable by the user**." Lesson (with a confidence caveat — this is an n = 1 corporate decision compatible with a resource-consolidation reading): **users don't reject proactivity; they reject proactivity they don't control.** Survivors in the layer: Huxe (ex-NotebookLM founders — daily private podcast from mail/calendar/news), Google Daily Listen, and **Kagi News — the anti-personalization route**: one bounded multi-source digest at noon, "news as a bounded ritual, not an endless habit" — proof that *restraint itself can be the selling point*. The layer has already rerun the 1994 Fishwrap fork — the one Sunstein later named Daily Me vs. Daily We.
- **Transformation layer (L2, the most mature)** — NotebookLM as the benchmark: sources → audio (four formats, 80+ languages, interruptible) / video / mind maps / reports; the team's core insight: **"transformation is fundamentally editorial"** — raw TTS goes unlistened; a consumption agent is an editor, not a format converter. **Particle.news — the closest company to this whole idea**: story-centric units (one Story aggregates hundreds of articles), Opposite Sides multi-perspective, ELI5, Podcast Clips; CEO's coinage "**liquid content**" — "content should adapt to users, not the other way around"; licensed revenue-share with Reuters/Fortune — creator relations as an explicit design goal. Arc Search's Browse-for-Me (the reviewer's line lands the taxonomy: "Comet gives you a worker; Arc gives you a **librarian**" — the librarian is the consumption-agent direction). The reader quartet: Readwise Reader (Ghostreader — on-demand transformation overlaid on the original), Matter (co-reader Q&A with footnotes), **Snipd** (AI chapters; its AI DJ is a design gem: **it never touches the original audio, it only drives the player** to the best 25% — engineering-level respect for the original form), ElevenReader (GenFM; absorbed the shuttered Omnivore — read-it-later being eaten by "listen to it now in another modality").
- **Enterprise & work layer (B2B willingness-to-pay: proven)** — Microsoft 365 Copilot "Catch up"; the enterprise-data-consumption quartet: Microsoft Fabric Data Agent (the official doc title literally reads "**Consume** a data agent from Copilot in Power BI" — consumption-side language is already in vendor documentation), Databricks Genie, Snowflake Intelligence, ThoughtSpot. Read precisely: these are L2-for-data (ask-the-numbers); attention orchestration and cross-domain consumption remain open — **the data domain has its L2; L1/L3 are as empty there as everywhere.**
- **Education & accessibility layer (deepest transformation, strongest legitimacy)** — Learn Your Way (+11 pp retention, the closest thing to an RCT for the whole thesis); Khanmigo (Sal Khan publicly names the Primer as the north star); Be My AI — the same world re-rendered for different sensory conditions; accessibility is the least contestable moral ground.
- **The platform control group ("their consumption agents" keep getting stronger)** — Meta's HSTU generative recommender (trillion-parameter, **+12.4% online** ◎), Kuaishou OneRec, Spotify AI DJ. The implication: the stronger *their* delegate gets, the scarcer *yours* becomes.

**Adjacent species, shared rails: agentic commerce** — Amazon Rufus/Buy-for-Me (closed-garden route; note the **Shopper Schism** frame from retail strategy: the *consumer* (who uses) and the *shopper* (who buys) are splitting into separate actors, and brands must build **machine-readable trust** — data integrity, fulfillment reliability, transparent policy — or become invisible to the algorithms that transact); OpenAI Instant Checkout ◎ (launched 2025-09 with Stripe, **terminated 2026-03-24**; first-party post-mortem: in-chat checkout converted ~3× worse than merchants' own sites, and merchants keep the register — the ACP protocol itself survives and evolves. Lesson: **users let agents research, not yet purchase — trust is granted per step**).

## 2. The casualty list (the highest-information table)

| Casualty | Outcome | Lesson |
|---|---|---|
| ChatGPT Pulse | ◎ sunset 2026-06-17 → removed 2026-07, folded into Scheduled Tasks | proactivity by silent inference < proactivity by explicit negotiation; "platform-scale general audience + fully proactive" does not compose |
| OpenAI Instant Checkout | ◎ terminated 2026-03-24 (protocol ACP survives) | no commercial loop of one's own; merchants won't cede checkout; trust is per-step |
| OpenAI Operator | folded into agent mode, 2025 | standalone "clicks web pages for you" is a feature, not a product |
| Recast | dead (farewell page only) ✅ | article→podcast pioneer; **single-point L2 cannot survive as a standalone subscription** while the same capability thrives inside platforms |
| Artifact | shut 2024-01, tech sold to Yahoo | single-point AI news app: no business model |
| Omnivore | shut 2024-11, team to ElevenLabs | read-it-later's endgame may be "consume now, in another modality" |
| Pocket | shut by Mozilla, 2025-07 ✅ | the classic read-it-later era closes |
| Humane AI Pin | servers off 2025-02; HP bought the assets ✅ | the "screens will be replaced" bet falsified; cloud dependence = brick on shutdown |
| Rabbit R1 | half-revived (rabbitOS 2 pivots to on-demand generated UI) ✅ | ship-then-patch nearly killed it; but "interfaces generated per context" got validated by the pivot |
| Limitless (née Rewind) | acquired by Meta 2025-12; sales stopped, service sunset ✅ | independent memory layers can't escape platform gravity — "your memories" can change owners overnight |
| Bee | acquired by Amazon 2025-07 ✅ | same lesson |
| New Computer "Dot" | closed ◐ | pure emotional-companion personal AI: moving story, no retention or revenue |
| PointCast / General Magic | prehistoric casualties (1990s) | right vision + wrong decade = a corpse; the preconditions differ this time (see Part 2 §2) |

**Three repeatedly-validated causes of death:** proactivity design (silent inference), commercial loop (none), independence (platform gravity). **Survivor pattern: every product still standing answers exactly one cause of death** — Ray-Ban (excel at the non-AI job first; present with subtraction), Snipd (respect the original form), Particle (build creator revenue-share in), Kagi (restraint as the product).

**A strategic correction on hardware** (restored from the source batches — the fusion draft had kept only the warnings): the casualty list vetoes *building* hardware, not the hardware wave itself. AI-native devices (audio glasses, earbuds, HUDs) **have no legacy screen and therefore structurally require consumption-side orchestration — a tailwind, not a threat**. The presentation-bandwidth ladder (voice-only → tiny generated UI → audio glasses → glanceable HUD) is the ready-made frame for choosing which device to parasitize.

## 3. The legal frontier (live, not settled)

**Amazon v. Perplexity** ◎ — the first "platform vs. buyer-side agent" case: sued 2025-11 (CFAA + California's CDAFA; Comet accused of masquerading as Chrome); preliminary injunction granted 2026-03 on the *Power Ventures* precedent — establishing "**user consent ≠ platform authorization**"; then — the fact most coverage misses — **the Ninth Circuit stayed the injunction pending appeal (2026-03-30); Comet runs as usual; oral argument held 2026-06-11; no ruling as of 2026-07-09**. The door marked "may an agent access a platform on the user's behalf" is neither open nor shut; it is *in court*.

A second legal dimension, mostly undiscussed: **adaptation rights**. Re-rendering touches the derivative-work right — a different and harder battlefield than "training is fair use," because renderings *substitute* for the original in the market. Private, ephemeral, non-distributed renderings are low-exposure (the "no re-emission" red line doubles as the legal firewall); commercial redistribution of renderings flips the risk live. Details in [Part 4, R11](04-risks-and-red-lines.md).

## 4. Open source: the bricks and the missing mortar

**Access layer:** RSSHub (~45k stars, "everything is RSSible"), NLWeb (any site gets `/ask` for humans + `/mcp` for agents, riding existing Schema.org/RSS), Web Applets (semantic action sets for agents — conceptually important), llms.txt (supply hot, demand cold — see §6).

**User-model layer (the largest gap):** Khoj (self-hosted second brain with scheduled delivery — a proactive skeleton already), mem0 (◐ ECAI 2025; vendor-reported LoCoMo leadership at ~7K tokens per retrieval — an earlier version of this page quoted a specific score of 92.5, which we could not verify against the paper and have withdrawn, 2026-07-09), Zep/Supermemory/OpenMemory MCP, Karakeep (self-hosted hoard-everything, already advertising agent operability), OpenBiliClaw (a precious folk prototype: local profiling of your cross-platform behavior, recommendation logic transparent and conversationally tunable), and the **HCP reference implementation** ◎ (see [Part 5](05-open-territory.md)).

**Rendering layer:** Folo (~38k stars — "the closest open-source thing to a consumption-agent portal"), Fabric (230+ crowd-sourced consumption patterns — a personal consumption pipeline in the terminal), Open Notebook (programmable Transformations module), Podcastfy, CopilotKit / assistant-ui / Vercel AI SDK (GenUI frameworks). Engineering consensus ◈: models should not emit raw HTML but **constrained structured specs** (JSON/DSL) rendered safely by design-system components — "generative UI ≠ arbitrary UI."

**RAG substrates** (AnythingLLM, Onyx, DocsGPT, Open WebUI, Letta): mature material-and-execution substrate, but Q&A-centric — **none treats consumption policy as a first-class citizen**. They shorten the MVP path; they do not change the gap analysis.

**The capability matrix (five batches, same conclusion):** across context sensing × preference modeling × modality transformation, **no row is complete**. The missing piece is the integration layer — the scheduling intelligence that knows *which brick, what form, right now*. The shortest prototype path is all existing bricks — `RSSHub → Folo/timeline → Fabric patterns → Podcastfy → Khoj` — and the mortar that is nowhere to be found is exactly the unified user model plus current-state estimation.

## 5. The academic map (seven themes, one line each)

① **Generative interfaces**: validated industrially (Google) and academically (Stanford, +72%), with boundary data — info-dense tasks gain most; chat remains fine for chat. ② **Sensemaking**: Pirolli & Card's dual-loop model is the theoretical foundation; Sensecape/Selenite show *semantic zoom* is the key UI principle; users refuse to hand over final synthesis (agency preservation has data). ③ **Proactive agents**: ProactiveBench / ProAgentBench make "when to interrupt" an evaluable question (When-to-Assist). ④ **LLM × recommendation**: surveys confirm "reshaping content representation" is still blank in RecSys — the field is stuck at *which item*, not *what shape*. ⑤ **Cognitive adaptation**: load-aware streaming (UIST 2025), LARF for reading difficulties, Auto-Slides (restructuring guided by learning science rather than vibes). ⑥ **Systems framing**: the *Agentic Web* paper's **Agent Attention Economy** (content now competes for agent invocations, not human clicks); *Software as Content* (2026-03) — the closest published neighbor to this framework. ⑦ **Risk empirics**: cognitive-debt EEG, DeepMind's 57-author ethics treatise, personalized fact-selection bias (Lazovich), the Accuracy Paradox (the more accurate, the more blindly trusted).

## 6. Protocols and the content economy

```
content/tools → [MCP]  (agents consuming tools & data; donated to Linux Foundation)
agent ↔ agent → [A2A]  (donated to LF; v1.0; 150+ orgs)
agent → human → [AG-UI events / A2UI declarative rendering / MCP Apps sandboxed iframes]  ← three in one year ✅
human's page ← [WebMCP]  (agents joining the page the human is on; Chrome origin trial)
content → agent → [NLWeb / llms.txt / Reuters' paid MCP server ◎]  (supply-side pipes)
money → content → [HTTP 402 / Pay-Per-Crawl → Pay-Per-Use / RSL 1.0 (1,500+ endorsers) / TollBit / ProRata attribution / Comet Plus three-way split / x402 stablecoin]
user context → [HCP — proposal, no adoption ◎]  ← the only layer with no grown standard
```

Four readings. **①** Three presentation protocols in one year, one of them (MCP Apps) co-drafted by Anthropic *and* OpenAI = industry consensus that plain-text answers are structurally insufficient — **the consumption side is an acknowledged, standalone problem domain**. Independent products should *reuse* this layer and differentiate on the taste model and loyalty architecture — do not invent a rendering protocol. **②** The settlement layer is a melee (per-crawl, per-use, per-attribution, subscription-pool); the winner takes the "Visa of the AI era" seat. Reality check: most AI bots today walk away from a 402 — willingness to pay remains the biggest open variable. **③** The llms.txt lesson (8.8× growth in adoption, **97% of files get zero requests** ◐): the supply side preps meals far faster than the demand side orders — **consumption agents at scale are the pump that makes every pipe flow**; that is the leverage seat. **④** The user-context layer is the only one without a standard; HCP's arrival re-grades it from "empty" to "proposed, unadopted" — and notably, HCP lives inside a *Loyal Agents* project: the loyalty line and the personal-memory line have already merged in the wild, exactly the pairing this framework treats as definitional.

**Business-model verdicts (five batches converged):** user subscription ★★★★★ (the only fully aligned model; ceiling constrained by historically weak willingness to pay for consumption tools) ｜ deduct-and-share ★★★★☆ (user prepays a content budget; the agent micro-pays creators per actual consumption via x402 — "the Spotify model of the agent era"; Comet Plus is the first prototype) ｜ self-hosted open source + paid hosting ★★★★★ (small but uncapturable) ｜ B2B licensing ★★★☆ (enterprises already pay for the cognitive cost of data consumption) ｜ commissions ★★ (inherent conflict; mandatory disclosure + isolation from ranking) ｜ **advertising ★ (history has already returned the verdict)**. **The pricing question is not "how much" — it is "who pays."**
